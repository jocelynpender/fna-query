Clean the data

The introduction status of a taxon provided by the Flora of North America is NA if no information has been provided by the treatment author. I am going to make the assumption that if no information has been provided, the plant is not introduced.

Let’s rename this value to something more readable.

ont_spp <- select(ont_spp, -X1) # Remove imported row indices
names(ont_spp) <- names(ont_spp) %>% tolower %>% sub(" ", "_", .) # clean up colnames

ont_spp$introduced <- ont_spp$introduced %>% replace_na(., "Native") %>% recode("TRUE" = "Introduced")

# Transform the data into long-format for ease of visualization
melted_ont_spp <- melt(ont_spp, id.vars = "taxon_name")

How many plants in Ontario are weedy?

Due to limitations in data capture, special status data are only available in in the following volumes: V3, V6, V9, V12, V17, V28.

I’d like to visualize what this means for my dataset in terms of data coverage.

volume_subset <- subset(melted_ont_spp, variable == "volume") %>% distinct

What is the likelihood is that the plant is introduced, given it is weedy?

I’d like to further investigate the relationship between weediness and introduction status.


ont_spp %>%
  summarize(weedy_prob = sum(special_status == "Weedy" & introduced == "Introduced", na.rm = TRUE)/sum(special_status == "Weedy", na.rm = TRUE))


ont_spp %>%
  summarize(prob = sum(introduced == "Introduced", na.rm = TRUE)/sum(introduced == "Introduced" | introduced == "Native", na.rm = TRUE))
NA

From the above, I see that being weedy greatly increases the probability that a plant is an introduced species. This makes sense given my understanding of what makes introduced species successful.

What is the taxonomic make-up of these two groups (introduced and weedy)?

Which families are the largest offenders?

Although by numbers, Asteraceae contributes the most number of introduced species to the Flora of Ontario, Caryophyllaceae is almost entirely comprised of introduced species. Rosaceae and Brassicaceae also contribute greatly in terms of numbers to the introduced flora, with Brassiceae being a family most dominated by introduced taxa.
